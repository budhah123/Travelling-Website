Rome

Rome, the capital of Italy, is a city of immense historical and cultural significance. Founded in 753 BC, it was the heart of the Roman Republic and later the Roman Empire, which was a major force in shaping Western civilization. The city's architectural heritage includes iconic structures such as the Colosseum, the Roman Forum, and the Pantheon, which reflect its ancient grandeur and influence.
Today, Rome also encompasses Vatican City, the world's smallest independent state and the spiritual center of the Roman Catholic Church. Vatican City is home to St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums, which include Michelangelo's famous Sistine Chapel.
Modern Rome is a vibrant metropolis that seamlessly integrates its rich historical past with contemporary life. It serves as Italy's political and administrative capital and is a major tourist destination, known for its beautiful piazzas, historic fountains, and lively cultural scene. The city's cuisine, featuring dishes like pasta carbonara and gelato, further enhances its appeal. Rome remains a dynamic city where ancient history and modern living converge.

Venice

Venice, a city in northeastern Italy, is renowned for its distinctive setting and historical grandeur. Founded over 1,500 years ago, Venice is situated on a network of 118 small islands in the Venetian Lagoon, separated by canals and connected by more than 400 bridges. The city's unique geography has led to its development on a series of narrow islands, with its main thoroughfare being the Grand Canal, which is navigated by gondolas and vaporetti (water buses) instead of traditional streets.
Venice is celebrated for its remarkable architecture and artistic heritage. Notable landmarks include St. Mark's Basilica, a stunning example of Byzantine architecture with its intricate mosaics and impressive domes; the Doge's Palace, a Gothic masterpiece that served as the seat of Venetian government; and the Rialto Bridge, one of the city's oldest and most famous bridges spanning the Grand Canal. The Piazza San Marco, often referred to as the city's "living room," is a central gathering place surrounded by historic buildings and bustling cafes.
The city’s labyrinthine network of canals and narrow streets contributes to its enchanting and romantic atmosphere, attracting millions of visitors each year. Venice is also renowned for its cultural events, such as the Venice Film Festival and the Carnival of Venice, known for its elaborate masks and costumes. Despite facing modern challenges like rising water levels and overtourism, Venice remains a symbol of artistic and architectural excellence, preserving its rich heritage while adapting to contemporary pressures. Its combination of historical splendor, unique urban layout, and vibrant cultural life makes it one of the most captivating cities in the world.

Changdeokgung Palace

Changdeokgung Palace, located in Waryong-dong, Jongno-gu, Seoul, is one of the five Royal Palaces of the Joseon dynasty (1392–1910), and still contains the original palace structures and other remains intact. It was built in 1405 as a Royal Villa but became the Joseon dynasty’s official Royal Residence after Gyeongbokgung, the original principal palace, was destroyed by fire in 1592 when Japanese forces invaded Korea. Thereafter, it maintained its prestigious position until 1867, when Gyeongbokgung was renovated and restored to its original status. Changdeokgung was listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1997.
Changdeokgung Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Seoul, is a serene escape into Korea’s royal history. Khajuraho, located in Madhya Pradesh, India, is famous for its exceptional group of temples renowned for their intricate and often erotic sculptures. Constructed between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples are masterpieces of Indian architecture and are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The complex is divided into three main groups: the Western Group, which includes the grand Kandariya Mahadev Temple and the detailed Lakshmana Temple; the Eastern Group, known for Jain temples such as the Parsvanatha and Adinatha Temples; and the Southern Group, featuring the Chaturbhuj and Duladeo Temples. The temples of Khajuraho are celebrated not only for their architectural beauty but also for their elaborate carvings that depict various aspects of life, including sensual theme.

As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Itsukushima Shrine is revered for its historical and cultural significance, situated on a sacred island that also features other historical sites and natural beauty, such as Mount Misen.
